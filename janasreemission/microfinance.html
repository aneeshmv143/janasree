Microfinance

One of the activities has been to promote Self-Help Groups (SHGs) of men and women to enable them to engage in thrift and use the collective savings to provide credit to each other on a mutual benefit basis. JSDM has a state Level Unit and 14 district level units, each of them registered as separate societies. JSDM has been acting as a Self Help Promoting Institution (SHPI) linking the SHGs to commercial bank branches to meet their credit requirements.

Though banks were extending credit to the SHGs, JSDM felt the need to promote its own organisation to ensure timely, unrestricted and repeat credit to the SHGs. Several options were considered for the appropriate legal form for the Micro Finance Institution. Finally it was decided to choose the legal form of a regular Non Banking Finance Company owned by the SHGs. To ensure ownership by the SHGs, and to channel funds through the SHG Federations, 14 MBTs were formed.

Janasree Sustainable Development Mission has promoted Janasree Micro Fin Limited (JMFL) a non banking finance company Micro Finance Institution (NBFC-MFI) registered under Indian Companies Act 1956 and working as per the guidelines and directives issued by RBI for NBFC-MFIs from time to time. JMFL has a paid up capital of Rs. 5 crore and has commenced its business in 2011. It is fully community owned NBFC-MFI.
